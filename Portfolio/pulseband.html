Process

As one of my first front-end development assignments, I quickly recognized the need to plan and outline the HTML structure of the page before anything else. Once the various sections and repeated headers and paragraph styles were listed, I moved onto defining element styles and found patterns that would be repeated throughout the page. This was helpful in ensuring my CSS remained dry. (Image 1. Original outlines shown right/above.)

While developing the page outline, I consulted the original designer and asked how he had envisioned two sections of the page that I knew were made to be dynamic, but were static on the PSD. He directed me to two websites, one with a timeline illustrated as an interactive SVG, and the other with iconic elements that flipped on hover. I took on the challenge to execute this site to his exact specifications and learned a lot about animating SVGs along the way!
